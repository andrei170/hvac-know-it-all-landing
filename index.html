The Twelve-Month Arc

Track A and Track B are sequential for the same operator. Launch Smarter in cycle one. Scale Smarter when the first hire is in and the route is stabilising. The cohort calendar is quarterly so the next cycle is never more than 90 days away.

Two tracks. Named work, week by week.

FAQ

Common questions, before applying.

How is the Business Track different from the free podcast and the free app?

The podcast, the YouTube channel, the Instagram, the Facebook HVAC Hub group, and the mobile app are the free tier. They stay free, they stay open, they stay the same cadence. The Business Track is the paid operator-development cohort - capped, application-gated, twelve-month arc across Track A and Track B. The two layers complement each other; they do not compete.

Is this ticketed trade training?

No. The Business Track is operator development for the shop-owner-track technician. Sales, pricing, ops, hiring, marketing, route optimisation, the owner-off-tools transition. It is not a substitute for TSSA gas training, EPA Section 608 refrigerant certification, the provincial trade colleges, or the manufacturer-certified programs. Trade-ticket curriculum stays where it is.

Will Gary promise a specific revenue number for my shop?

No. Anyone selling a guaranteed first-year shop revenue in the trades is selling something else. The cohort sells operator development - the pricing structure, the cash plan, the hire checklist, the route plan, the owner-off-tools transition. Results vary by market, capital, operator execution, and regulatory environment.

What is the time commitment?

Track A is 8 weeks. Track B is 6 weeks. Tuesday evening cohort call (60-75 minutes). Friday office-hours block (optional, open seating). Cohort channel open 7 days a week. Work between calls is 3-6 hours per week. Some weeks lighter (audit weeks), some weeks heavier (build weeks).

What is the investment?

Mid-four-figure pricing per track. The application surfaces the exact cycle pricing on submit. The tier is sized for the operator ready to do the work - the room is not entry-level.

Which track do I apply to?

The 90-second scorecard returns the band - Too Early, Ready For Track A, Ready For Track B, Too Far Along. The band tells you which track fits today plus the three biggest gaps in front of you right now. The scorecard sits underneath the homepage VSL.

What if I am too early?

The application routes you back to the free content, the app, the podcast, and the HVAC Hub Facebook group. Save the scorecard, retake it in 12 months when the foundation is in. Too early is honest, not a knock.

What if I am too far along (10-plus techs)?

The cohort sizes for the launch / 1-to-5-tech operator. At 10-plus techs, the right peer-network rooms are a better fit. The application surfaces the routing - reply "rooms" to the routing email and the team sends the list.

Canada, US, or international?

The cohort welcomes operators across North America. Provincial / state licensing is your responsibility - the cohort covers the licensing-audit module by province / state, not as a substitute for the regulator. International applicants are reviewed case by case for time-zone fit.

Refund policy?

Refund policy locks per cycle and surfaces on the application page for the cycle you apply to.

Why do I have to apply?

The cohort caps at 25-30 per cycle to keep the room real. The application sizes the room, checks the fit, and surfaces the pricing for the cycle. Four minutes. Manual review within 48 hours. Approval triggers the enrolment link direct.
